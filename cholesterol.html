What is Cholesterol?

Cholesterol is a type of fat found in your blood that is necessary for building cells, producing hormones, and digesting fats. However, too much bad cholesterol (LDL) can lead to heart disease and stroke.

Liver Profile & Uric Acid: Liver health directly affects cholesterol levels. Elevated uric acid can increase risk of cardiovascular diseases. Maintaining healthy liver function helps balance cholesterol naturally.

Why Cholesterol Control is Important

High cholesterol levels increase the risk of plaque formation in arteries, which can cause heart attack or stroke. Proper diet, lifestyle, and natural remedies help maintain healthy cholesterol levels and improve overall cardiovascular health.

Common Cholesterol-Related Disorders

  • High LDL cholesterol (bad cholesterol)
  • Low HDL cholesterol (good cholesterol)
  • Hyperlipidemia
  • Atherosclerosis
  • Coronary artery disease
